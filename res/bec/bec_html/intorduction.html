Nonequilibrium Phase Transitions in Evolutionary Dynamics

Illustration of Evolutionary Game Process. Diffrent colors are gains of each player. New players join the game every time step. x-axis: time, y axis: gains.

Co-existence of players: each area obeys Bose Distribution.

Dominance by a single player is described by Bose-Einstein Condensation.

In 18th century, Thomas Malthus argued that "Population, when unchecked, increases in a geometrical ratio. Subsistence only increases in an arithmetical ratio." The statement is now known as Malthusian population model. According to this hypthesis, the check is inevitable. At the beginning of the last centruy, the logistic hypothesis, that assumes nonlinear saturation factor for the exponential growth, has been extensively investigated. These include the Lotka-Volttera equation of competition and that of predaytor and prey. The former was confirmed by the laboratory experiments of Paramecium conducted by G. F. Gauze. Incidentally, it was pointed out by Robert May that its discrete one-dimensional model (the logistic map), a model for insect population dynamics, shows chaotic behaviour. The equation has provided theoretical grounds for the chaos in the dynamical systems. However, if more than two species are involved in the differential equations, no analytical solution is avalble. Furthermore, natural competitions include many species, and allows the successive intruduction of new species, which is an whole mark of nonequilibrium system.

We developed a theory in which we can treat natural competition as a many-body problem, thus by statistical mechanics. Most importantly, the model is based on the Malthusian population model. In this model, instead of the logistic hypothesis, we introduce normalizatoin processes. Every time step the population that grows exponentially normalizes to a finte number. It is a game process in which players compete for gains (money, food, market share...). The rules are very simple.

At each time step:

  1. A players' gain is proportional to its ability and its gain at the previous step.
  2. A new player joins the competition.
  3. Players compete for a fixed total amount of resources.

The analogy implies that gains obey unique characteristics of Bose statistics; gains (money, food, market share...) have statistical tendency to gather. As expected, the system shows phase transition analogous to Bose-Einstein condensation, which draws natural analogy of dominance and evolution as condensates in the process. Further analysis revealed that this competitive process contains substantial features which were already observed in the research of biological evolution. These are competitive exclusion principle and punctuated equilibrium. The former states that the only one species can win in one niche, and the latter is the observations of step-like evolution; evolution occurs discontinuously and there are equilibria between them. More interestingly, the theory predicts that increasing mutation rate decelerates and eventually stops evolution. As mutation is considered to be engine of evolution, this is apparently counterintuitive. However, this negative effect is actually observed in laboratory experiments.
